Deckhouse is created by Flant — the company that boasts a rich history of running Kubernetes in production since 2016. Deckhouse was born as our in-house effort to deal with the challenges of creating and maintaining dozens — later, hundreds — of full-featured, production-grade, robust Kubernetes clusters of different sizes. Our Open Source

We adhere to the Open Source ideology since the inception of the company.

Creating Open Source software for a decade, we also released two precursors to Deckhouse (in 2019). They are used under the hood and well-known in the Kubernetes community (e.g. check this talk at KubeCon NA 2020).

Shell-operator

A handy tool to easily create your Kubernetes operator using scripts (Bash, Python, etc.) or any binaries.

Addon-operator

A system to manage additional components in your Kubernetes clusters in a consistent & automated way.

Other Open Source projects by Flant include

werf

A powerful CI/CD tool to glue Git, Docker, container registry, your existing CI system, Helm, and Kubernetes

grafana-statusmap

A Grafana panel plugin to visualize the status of multiple objects over time.

k8s-image-availability-exporter
